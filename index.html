How I think / what the other side taught me

Most analysts have to learn the business context on the job. I brought it with me. Five years as an operator converts into three things you can't get from a certification:

Owning a sales target taught me which numbers people are actually judged on. So my dashboards lead with the metrics someone's job depends on, not the ones that are easiest to chart.
Sitting through budget meetings taught me that every chart must survive a "so what?" If a visual doesn't point at an action, it's decoration. I cut it.
Running delivery taught me where data gets dirty, because I was the one entering it. I don't trust a clean-looking table. I know what shortcuts, workarounds, and Friday-evening entries look like from the inside, and I check for them first.
I don't just crunch numbers. I help solve problems.
